# Open Challenges

## 1. Interventional Validity

A simulated future can look realistic while being wrong about the effect of an intervention. Medical world models need stronger causal and interventional validation.

## 2. Long-Horizon Error Accumulation

Autoregressive clinical simulations may drift over time. Long-horizon state retention and trajectory consistency must be reported.

## 3. Multimodal State Construction

Clinical state is rarely contained in one modality. Useful systems need to integrate imaging, text, labs, treatments, time intervals, and patient-specific context.

## 4. Uncertainty Calibration

Treatment planning and clinical decision support require calibrated uncertainty, not only point predictions.

## 5. Biological and Anatomical Plausibility

Generated images or cellular responses must obey anatomical, physiological, and biological constraints.

## 6. Reproducibility

Many strong medical world-model papers still lack public code, datasets, pretrained weights, or standardized benchmarks.

## 7. Clinical Safety

Medical world models should be framed as research systems unless validated prospectively and integrated with human oversight.
